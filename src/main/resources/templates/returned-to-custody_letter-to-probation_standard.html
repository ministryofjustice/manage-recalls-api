You will NOT be reminded to submit the papers and if the Report and Plan are not received by the deadline this will delay the Parole Board’s review of suitability for re-release.  In these cases your manager and/or ACO will be notified of the delay.

Please note that it is extremely important that you give a clear recommendation on whether or not re-release is supported before or after day 28 of return to custody, so that the Public Protection Casework Section can make a decision on whether or not the offender should be re-released into the community.

If re-release is supported, prompt submission of the reports will enable the Secretary of State to consider the case for executive re-release and discuss with yourself if appropriate. The report should include details of accommodation with dates of availability (if available for a limited time only please highlight this to the PPCS caseworker) and full details of any proposed licence conditions.  Where the offender requires a prescription for methadone or any other type of medication please ensure that you are very clear about the timescales required to secure that ahead of any actual release date.

In addition, please be mindful that you may need to liaise with other agencies, for example, in respect of MAPPA or PPO initiatives or with the Victim Liaison Officer. Where appropriate, this will need to be fed into the Risk Management Plan.

Please be aware that, in the event that the Parole Board decide to make no recommendation for release, the information provided by you in the Risk Management Report will be used as part of an initial assessment to consider whether the offender has sufficient time before their Sentence Expiry Date (SED) to reduce their risk of harm and reoffending and be safely re-released into the community on licence.
